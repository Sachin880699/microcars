Microlino

Microlino is a compact electric microcar developed by Micro Mobility Systems, Switzerland. Inspired by the iconic BMW Isetta, Microlino is designed for modern urban transportation with minimal footprint, high efficiency, and low environmental impact.

The car is ideal for short city commutes, offering weather protection, easy parking, and very low energy consumption compared to traditional cars. Its front-opening door design makes it unique and extremely practical in tight urban spaces.

Technical Details

  • Vehicle Name: Microlino
  • Vehicle Type: Electric Microcar (L7e Category)
  • Powertrain: Fully Electric
  • Drive Type: Rear-Wheel Drive
  • Seating Capacity: 2 Passengers
  • Top Speed: Up to 90 km/h
  • Battery Options: 6 kWh / 10.5 kWh / 14 kWh
  • Driving Range: 125 – 230 km (variant dependent)
  • Charging Method: Standard AC household socket
  • Energy Consumption: Very low (urban optimized)

Dimensions

  • Length: ~2,519 mm
  • Width: ~1,473 mm
  • Height: ~1,501 mm
  • Wheelbase: ~1,728 mm
  • Weight: ~500 – 525 kg

Key Features

  • Front-opening door for easy entry
  • Compact size for urban parking
  • Fully electric and zero emissions
  • Digital instrument cluster
  • Panoramic roof (variant dependent)
  • Regenerative braking
  • Low running and maintenance cost

Origin & Philosophy

  • Country of Origin: Switzerland
  • Manufacturer: Micro Mobility Systems
  • Design Philosophy: Minimalism, efficiency, sustainability
  • Target Market: Urban commuters
